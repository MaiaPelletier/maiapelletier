I used ggbeeswarm to visualize the results of all the songs we tested ourselves on. Where did we do better/worse? Do our results mirror each other‚Äôs or do they vastly differ?

set.seed(2016)
p <- 
  pudding_data %>%
  ggplot(aes(year, knowledge)) +
  geom_beeswarm(aes(color = name),
                groupOnX = FALSE,
                show.legend = F) +
  labs(x = NULL, y = NULL) +
  facet_wrap(.~name, ncol = 2) +
  scale_x_continuous(breaks = scales::pretty_breaks()) +
  scale_color_pjo(name = NULL)
p

Throwing a geom_smooth() on the plot, using method = loess, is helpful to see the years where we did better/worse.

  • At a quick glance, our results look like they are fairly similar (as you would expect of 2 people born in the same year).

  • You can see there‚Äôs an immediate increase in music recognition in the 2000s where we started to actually recognize the music we grew up with.

  • In general, it looks like J‚Äôs ‚Äúdon‚Äôt know it‚Äù is more saturated than mine. This could mean that I know more music :smirk:. However, where I listened to probably around ~30 seconds of each song, he was quick to click ‚Äúdon‚Äôt know it‚Äù after about 10 seconds, so this could also mean he missed parts of a song that he might have recognized.

  • There was a lot of stuff that sounded familiar to me but I couldn‚Äôt quite place, especially in the 1980s-1990s.

  • When we hit the 2000s, things stopped ‚Äúsounding familiar‚Äù to me and I either just knew the song or I didn‚Äôt. Whereas J had songs that he couldn‚Äôt quite place over all the decades.

  • I think I was more liberal with the singing the lyrics option since to me generally knowing a song = knowing the lyrics. Considering that, J and I had pretty similar knowledge of the 2000s and 2010s.

I was curious to see what music we knew from decade to decade (aka which decades can I boast more music knowledge than J), so I aggregated the ‚Äúknow it‚Äùs and the ‚Äúsinging it‚Äùs to visualize this.

decade_knowledge <- 
  pudding_data %>%
  mutate(knowledge2 = ifelse(
    knowledge %in% c("don't know it", "sounds famililar"),
    "don't know it",
    "know it"
    )) %>%
  filter(knowledge2 == "know it") %>% 
  count(name, year_dec) %>%
  mutate(percent = n/30)

decade_knowledge %>%
  ggplot(aes(year_dec, percent)) +
  geom_col(aes(fill = name), 
           position = "dodge",
           width = 4) +
  labs(x = NULL, y = "% of songs known") +
  scale_y_continuous(labels = scales::percent_format()) +
  scale_x_continuous(
    breaks = c(1960, 1970, 1980, 1990, 2000, 2010),
    labels = c('60s', '70s', '80s', '90s', '00s', '10s')
    ) +
  scale_fill_pjo(name = NULL) +
  theme(legend.margin = margin(b = 5, unit = 'mm'),
        legend.key.width = unit(2, 'mm'))

  • J is not at all familiar with the music in the 90s, only recognizing <15% of songs. This is a little surprising but also understandable I guess since we were born in the mid-late 90s.

  • I did surprisingly ok in the 60s (recognizing over 50% of songs!). Definitely not well üòÖ, but I was expecting that to be my worst decade. It turned out that I recognized the least music from the 70s (also a bit surprising to me, I thought the 70s would be my best decade pre-2000).

The only category where J came close to beating me was in the 70s, where we tied. So all in all, I think I get to pretty decisively take the win in all the decades otherwise!:trophy:
